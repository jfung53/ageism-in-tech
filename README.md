
# WIP - Women and Ageism in Tech Occupations

Data analysis project exploring ageism and sexism in tech jobs using employment data in the US.  

Gender disparities in information technology occupations surrounding education, salaries, and hiring practices are well-documented as unfriendly to women.  There is lots of evidence that women returning to tech jobs (or any job) after having children consistently face structural obstacles and salary penalties.  But as women in tech age past child-bearing years and reach the mid-to-late stages of their careers, there is a narrative that older women choose to leave the industry, falling away rapidly while men gain ground.  If tech workers are considered “old” and are perceived to have more challenges learning new things by the time they reach 35, I’m curious how this intersects with gender in these male-dominated spaces and how much choice is involved, as opposed to involuntary attrition.  I’m interested in measuring whether older women, compared to other groups, face more discrimination when looking for work after involuntary job losses.  While many women do, in fact, choose to leave tech jobs as they age, my hypothesis is that those who want to stay face a greater amount of hiring discrimination, and I would like to try quantifying this.  

## Research Question
Do age and sex predict employment status in tech jobs after involuntary job loss?  In particular, were older women disproportionately affected by the mass tech layoffs in 2022-2023?

So far I've looked at data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, IPUMS CPS, and the Pew Research Center.  

This project is a work in progress, stay tuned!
